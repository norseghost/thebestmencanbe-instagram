Recent research suggests that as many as 12,200 current and former Boy Scouts of America have been abused by 7,819 troop leaders and other volunteers. In an effort to protect themselves from the fallout of this research and inevitable lawsuits, the Boy Scouts are considering filing for bankruptcy protection, despite having assets totaling more than $1 billion. Doing this would mean new abuse claims would no longer be handled in state courts, while those filing claims would have to get in line with other creditors.
