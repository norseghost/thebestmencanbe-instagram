When Stephanie Montgomery told management at the strip club where she worked that she was raped, they did nothing. The police weren’t much help either. “Nothing was going to happen. No justice, just another rape, the world moved on,” Kathy Dobie writes for California Saunday. “The #MeToo movement had opened up the conversation, sure, and it had also spurred men into hyperdefensiveness and aggression, but when the smoke cleared, had anything really changed? Where were the arrests, the convictions? Did a stripper have a bigger voice, a better shot at justice than she would’ve two or five or twenty years ago?”⁣
