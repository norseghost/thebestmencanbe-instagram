For some, heels are manmade torture-devices. For others, a necessary evil. But over 19,000 women in Japan have come out in support of a movement they call #KuToo, which asks that the unofficial rule of women having to wear high heels at the workplace be struck down. It all began when actress and freelance writer Yumi Ishikawa tweeted about how she was expected to wear heels even though she worked in a funeral parlour. Her tweet venting about this went viral and now Japan's got its own protest movement that references #MeToo and combines the Japanese words for shoes (kutsu) and pain (kutsuu).
